They are delicate with long legs and tiny, round heads composed mostly of the eyes (some appearing to have three eyes - two lower ones and a top central one). Many have an eye bridge on the top of the head, and most have simple eyes. Their antennae are fairly characteristic, usually being long and curved in life and composed of fuzzy, teardrop-shaped segments (looking like feathery pipe cleaners). They have very few wing veins and their wings are often covered in setae. Larval Cecidomyiidae have a greatly reduced head that is mainly fleshy with small antennae and two, internal, stylet-like mandibles. A common feature on larval gall midges is a sternal spatula, a hardened, usually Y-shaped rod found under their head on their "chest".
