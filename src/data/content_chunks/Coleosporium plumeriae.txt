).Symptoms and SignsMany orange pustules of uredinia on the underside of leaves are the most common sign of this rust. They are never present on the flowers or stems. Yellow spotting is also visible on the upper surface of affected leaves, in which numerous pustules are visible on the lower surface. As disease progresses, the spots may enlarge and merge to form sunken brown spots. Infected leaves may curl and eventually drop (2).  Teliospores are four-celled and orange, while urediniospores are lighter yellow in color and borne on erumpent uredinia, bursting through the lower leaf surface (1).Ecology and SpreadVery wet and humid conditions are ideal for plumeria rust development.C. plumeriaeis spread by windborne urediniospores on wet leaves in humid conditions. The spores are able to germinate on and infect the upper leaf surface with free water available and a conducive environment.Geographic DistributionPlumeria rust has been reported in most of Central America, the West Indies, and the United States of America (Louisiana and Florida) (3).ManagementResistant species ofPlumeriaare available and should be planted. Cultural control practices include planting plumeria in drier areas and spacing the plants further apart to aerate the canopy and dry the leaf surface quicker. If disease is present, all fallen diseased leaves should be destroyed as these are sources of infection for the next season. Chemical controls are available, but fungicides should be rotated to inhibit resistance buildup. Mycobutanil and azoxystrobin are two active ingredients that are registered for management of this disease (4). Yellow spotting is also visible on the upper surface of affected leaves, in which numerous pustules are visible on the lower surface. As disease progresses, the spots may enlarge and merge to form sunken brown spots. Infected leaves may curl and eventually drop (2).  Teliospores are four-celled and orange, while urediniospores are lighter yellow in color and borne on erumpent uredinia, bursting through the lower leaf surface (1).Ecology and SpreadVery wet and humid conditions are ideal for plumeria rust development.C. plumeriaeis spread by windborne urediniospores on wet leaves in humid conditions. The spores are able to germinate on and infect the upper leaf surface with free water available and a conducive environment.Geographic DistributionPlumeria rust has been reported in most of Central America, the West Indies, and the United States of America (Louisiana and Florida) (3).ManagementResistant species ofPlumeriaare available and should be planted. Cultural control practices include planting plumeria in drier areas and spacing the plants further apart to aerate the canopy and dry the leaf surface quicker. If disease is present, all fallen diseased leaves should be destroyed as these are sources of infection for the next season. Chemical controls are available, but fungicides should be rotated to inhibit resistance buildup. Mycobutanil and azoxystrobin are two active ingredients that are registered for management of this disease (4).
