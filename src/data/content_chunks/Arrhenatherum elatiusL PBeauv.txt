Arrhenatherum elatius(L.) P.Beauv. - False Oat-grass  -Gramineae / Poaceae© Paul Hackney© Alan Hill(Map updated: March 2008)This tall, conspicuous wild native grass has flowers similar in appearance to those of oats (but smaller). It is very common in a variety of grassy habitats.There are two subspecies or varieties: one has swollen bases to the main shoots, like small onion bulbs, and is sometimes called 'onion couch'; the other lacks these swellings. Both varieties appear to be equally abundant in Northern Ireland.All names:Arrhenatherum elatius(L.) P.Beauv.;Arrhenatherum avenaceumBeauv.;Arrhenatherum bulbosum(Willd.)
