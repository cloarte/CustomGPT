 Invasion: In Australia,C. equisetifoliasubsp.equisetifoliais mainly a small tree, 8-16 m tall, in contrast to populations in South-East Asia where the tree may attain heights of 35 m with diameters to 50 cm. The subsp.incanais typically a small tree and may be reduced to only a large shrub 6-10 m tall on poor sites. Form in wild populations is very variable, varying from crooked, low branching trees on exposed sea shores, to straight-stemmed forest trees in more sheltered situations. Crowns are finely branched.
