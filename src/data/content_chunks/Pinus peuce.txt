ion: Physiology and Phenology Pinus peuce is pollinated by wind. The cones have a two-year cycle of development. In the beginning, they are green with their scales closed and covered with resin granules. They mature in September-October of the second year. The seeds shed as soon as cones mature in September-October, 17-18 months after pollination ( Alexandrov and Andonovski, 2011 ). Longevity Pinus peuce is a perennial, long-lived tree that under exceptional conditions can reach the age of 675 years, but generally trees live between 200-300 years ( Zafirov et al., 2020 ). Activity Patterns Pinus peuce growth is positively influenced by high temperatures at the end of the previous growing season and negatively affected by cold winters. In some areas, trees may be negatively impacted by drought during the hottest summer months (especially in August; Zafirov et al., 2020 ). Population Size and Density Only 17-20 subpopulations of P. peuce remain in the wild across the Balkans ( Farjon, 2017 ). Associations Across its native range, P. peuce can be found associated with Picea abies , Abies alba and Fagus sylvatica . At the upper tree line in the Balkans, it can form mixed forest stands with Pinus heldreichii ( Jankoviƒá et al., 1987 ; Stevanoviƒá et al., 1995 ; Alexandrov and Andonovski, 2011 ). Environmental Requirements Pinus peuce is adapted to grow at high elevations (range: 600 m - 2300 m), in areas characterized by cold and high humidity mountain climate. It is well adapted to grow on a variety of soil types, including limestone, dolomite, silicate and carbonate soils, as well as soils poor in nutrients.
