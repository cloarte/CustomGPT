Cladium mariscus(L.) Pohl - Great Fen-sedge  -Cyperaceae© Graham Day© Ralph Forbes© Ralph Forbes(Map updated: March 2008)© Alan HillA tall (to six feet) and robust species with long, leathery sawtooth-edged leaves, and producing clusters of red-brown flower spikes. It forms dense beds in fens, usually in lowland situations. Most common in the south and west of Northern Ireland, especially the Lough Erne basin.The original name for this plant is simplysedge- a name and usage which comes from the Fens of East Anglia, but botanists have appropriated the word for many of the related plants in the family Cyperaceae.All names:Cladium mariscus(L.)
