Physiology and Phenology Trypodendron domesticum attacks stressed, dying trees or recently dead trees or logs ( Jackson et al., 2011 ). The insects are attracted to hardwoods by the volatile chemicals produced by the phloem and sapwood when the trees are weakened or enter initial stages of decay ( Gaubicher et al., 2003 ). Activity Patterns Adults of T. domesticum occur year-round, overwintering in galleries on trunks of the host species ( Jackson et al., 2011 ). The species is reported as being univoltine ( Jackson et al. 2011 ), but also as bivoltine with two generations, or a sister generation, each year in parts of Europe ( Parini and Petercord, 2006 ; UK Beetles, 2021 ). According to Parini and Petercord (2006) , T. domesticum has a first flight out of the galleries by mid- to end of March. It might be followed by a second flight of the stragglers, due to an interruption caused by unfavourable atmospheric conditions from the middle of April to the beginning of May. A third flight can be seen regularly from the end of May to the beginning of June. The authors argue that this third flight is from a possible second generation, and indicative of the species being bivoltine in central to south-eastern Europe.
