Carum verticillatum(L.) Koch - Whorled Caraway  -Umbelliferae / Apiaceae© Roy Anderson© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)A relative of caraway (Carum carvi), but this species is an Irish native plant and has no culinary or herbal uses. It occurs in marshy ground in a few places in north Antrim and the northern part of Co. Londonderry. The leaves are finely divided with the segments arranged in whorls (rings) around the main leaf midrib.All names:Carum verticillatum(L.)
