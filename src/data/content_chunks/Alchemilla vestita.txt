Alchemilla vestita(Buser) Raunk. - lady's-mantle  -Rosaceae(Map updated: March 2008)One of three very similar native species of lady's mantle (the others areAlchemilla xanthochloraandA. glabra) which are found in grassy places. The flowers are tiny and greenish-yellow.AStems and leaf-stalks densely covered with spreading hairs. Leaf hairy on both upper and underside:Alchemilla vestitaBStems and leaf stalks covered with spreading hairs. Leaf hairy only on underside:Alchemilla xanthochlora.COnly the lowest 1 - 2 internodes hairy, with adpressed hairs (not spreading hairs). Upper part of stem hairless.
