The Daniel E. Salmon Internship Program
Are you a student in a nonveterinary field of study? We have paid 
internships—with tuition assistance—available at the U.S. Department of 
Agriculture’s (USDA) Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS). 
Our interns work alongside APHIS veterinarians and other professionals  
in a wide range of fields to protect American agriculture.
Who Is Daniel E. Salmon?
Dr. Salmon was founding chief 
of the USDA’s Bureau of Animal 
Industry, predecessor of today’s 
Veterinary Services. As a member 
of Cornell University’s first class of 
graduates, he earned his bachelor’s 
degree in veterinary medicine in 
1872, and 4 years later, Cornell 
awarded him the first Doctorate 
of Veterinary Medicine degree in 
the country. Today, Dr. Salmon 
is best known for identifying the 
infectious pathogen Salmonella 
and pioneering the fight against 
contagious diseases. His work 
contributed immeasurably to  
improving public health and  
disease control efforts.
Internship Experiences
As a Daniel E. Salmon intern, 
you’ll experience the real-world 
situations facing Federal animal 
health employees every day. This 
may include work in the field, 
laboratories, and office settings. 
In addition to veterinarians, some 
of our key positions include 
microbiologists, entomologists, 
and budget and management 
analysts. We offer a wide range 
of learning opportunities, 
mentorship, and career 
development throughout the 
program so our interns are ready
for life in the Federal

An Exciting Career Pathway
The USDA Pathways Program 
offers different “pathway” 
opportunities for students and 
recent graduates to work in 
agriculture and many other  
fields. One of these pathways is 
the USDA Internship Program. 
APHIS Veterinary Services 
participates in this program, 
offering the Daniel E. Salmon 
Internship to recruit talented 
professionals. This internship is 
expected to last more than 1 year, 
and the intern may be converted 
to a permanent position with 
APHIS after completing the 
Pathways Program and meeting 
all other requirements.

After completing all program requirements, 
you must become a full-time APHIS 
employee for at least 1 calendar year for 
each school year the internship covered. If 
you do not accept an APHIS employment 
offer, you must reimburse all financial 
assistance received under the program. 
If you fail to serve the entire length of the 
mandatory APHIS employment period, you 
must reimburse the agency a prorated share 
of the funds awarded. 
Eligibility
Upon entrance on duty, you must:
1. Be a U.S. citizen;
2. Meet the educational and curriculum 
requirements of the internship position 
for which you apply. These requirements 
and the specific duties of the position 
will be stipulated in each internship 
announcement and may include a wide 
range of majors or fields of study;  
3. Be enrolled as a half-time or full-time 
student and/or accepted for enrollment 
in an accredited college or university 
within the United States;* 
4. Be in good academic standing; and  
5. Agree to work for the agency during 
school breaks for a minimum of 640 
hours prior to completion of studies. 
Positions are available in many locations.

How To Apply
Interested students must apply to specific 
internship announcements online via  
www.usajobs.gov. Locate announcements  
by entering the key words “Daniel E. Salmon” 
in the search engine under the “Student and 
Recent Graduates” tab. You must have a 
USAJOBS account to apply. 
Required Documents
The online application system will prompt  
you to answer self-assessment questions  
and to submit the following: 
1. Résumé
2. Transcripts for all completed  
college work to date
3. One letter of recommendation  
from college officials
