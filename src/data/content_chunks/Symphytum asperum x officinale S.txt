Symphytum asperum x officinale (S. x uplandicum)Nyman - Russian Comfrey  -Boraginaceae© Neville McKee© Graham Day© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)A tall, rank, bristly herbaceous perennial with blue, violet or purple tubular flowers and (normally) no wing running down the stem, or the wing poorly developed. A common plant of waste ground, rubbish dumps, roadsides etc.; formerly grown as a fodder plant. Generally more frequent thanS. officinale.This is the hybrid betweenSymphytum officinale, native to the British Isles andS. asperumfrom SW Asia. The poorly developed stem wing is one of the characters that distinguishes it fromS. officinale, but like most hybrids it is very variable and shows various combinations of the characters of the two parent species.All names:Symphytum asperum x officinale (S. x uplandicum)Nyman;Symphytum x uplandicumNyman;Symphytum asperum x officinale;Symphytum peregrinumauct.;Symphytum lilacinumauct.;Symphytum densiflorumauct.;Symphytum caeruleumauct.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
