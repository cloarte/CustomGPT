Myriophyllum verticillatumL. - Whorled Water-milfoil  -Halogaraceae(Map updated: March 2008)Water milfoils are submerged aquatic species with feathery leaves, although the flower spikes are held above the water. The flowers are small and unremarkable.All occur in still or slow-moving water (lakes, ponds, flooded quarries, drainage ditches, slow streams etc).Myriophyllum verticillatumis very rare except around Lough Erne in Co Fermanagh.All names:Myriophyllum verticillatumL.;Myriophyllum pectinatumDC.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
