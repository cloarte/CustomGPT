Cardamom
Zingiberaceae
Elettaria cardamomum
(L.) Maton.
Amomum cardamomum
L.
Source:
Magness et al. 1971
Both the above related species of tropical plants produce the cardamom
seeds of commerce. Both are tropical, perennial herbs, the tops growing each
year from underground rhizomes.
E. cardamomum
reaches 5 to 10 feet, with 
lanceolate leaves up to 2 feet long. The capsules are oblong to globular,
ribbed and indehiscent. These dried capsules are the principal cardamoms of
commerce. The seeds and plant of
A. cardamomum
are very similar to 
"grains of paradise" These seeds are also 
sold as cardamom.
