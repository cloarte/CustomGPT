Alchemilla glabraNeyg. - lady's-mantle  -Rosaceae© Paul Hackney(Map updated: March 2008)One of three very similar native species of lady's mantle (the others areAlchemilla xanthochloraandAlchemilla vestita) which are found in grassy places. This is the only completely hairless species, The flowers are tiny and greenish-yellow.AStems and leaf-stalks densely covered with spreading hairs. Leaf hairy on both upper and underside:Alchemilla vestitaBStems and leaf stalks covered with spreading hairs. Leaf hairy only on underside:Alchemilla xanthochlora.COnly the lowest 1 - 2 internodes hairy, with adpressed hairs (not spreading hairs). Upper part of stem hairless.
