Gray - Amphibious Bistort  -Polygonaceae© Graham Day© Ralph Forbes© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)© Graham Day© Ian Dodkins'Amphibious' refers to the ability of this native species of bistort to grow in either aquatic or terrestrial habitats, often both at the same site such as in lakes and on their dry shores. In water, the leaves float on the surface. The flowers are small and pink in small spikes. It is common and widespread across Northern Ireland around lakes, rivers and canals, especially in the south and east.An alternative name isPolygonum amphibium.All names:Persicaria amphibia(L.)
