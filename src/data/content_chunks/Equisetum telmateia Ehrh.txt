Equisetum telmateiaEhrh. - Great Horsetail  -Equisetaceae© Paul Hackney© Paul Hackney© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)© Paul HackneyThis is the largest of the horsetails. In early spring it produces new unbranched whitish shoots which terminate in large spore-producing cones. These are followed by the vegetative shoots which when mature have a central upright whitish main stem and a large number of long, fine, drooping side-branches. The plant is frequent on hedgebanks and slopes across Northern Ireland, especially common near the coast in the east and in Co Fermanagh.All names:Equisetum telmateiaEhrh.;Equisetum maximumauct.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
