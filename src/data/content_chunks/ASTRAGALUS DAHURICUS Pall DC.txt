ASTRAGALUS DAHURICUS (Pall) DC. Family: leguminosae Common name: Milk Vetch. Leguminosae Ecol. & Biol: Perennial. Stem: erect, angled. Leaves: compound with 8-13 pairs of small, oval, bluntish, smooth leaflets, lower sides hairy. Inflorescence: flower - bluish purple, in dense raceme. Propagation by seed. Flowering time: July-September. Distribution: Common weed in the uncropped area, fodder crops and vegetables. More common in forest steppe zone of Mongolia. Control measures: Cultivation in May followed by post-emergence herbicides and again deep cultivation in August-September can help to reduce its infestation in subsequent seasons.
