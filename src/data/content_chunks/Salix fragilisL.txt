Salix fragilisL. - Crack Willow  -Salicaceae© Alan Hill© Alan Hill© Alan Hill(Map updated: March 2008)Crack willow is a very common species of lowland damp or wet places such as river banks, marshy ground and lake shores. It can grow to about 50 ft (15m) in height, but tends to have an untidy growth habit. The twigs break off easily at their junctions with the larger branches. The leaves are long and narrow with small teeth along their edges.
