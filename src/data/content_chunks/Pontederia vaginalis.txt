The optimum temperatures for seed germination are between 25 to 35¬∞C, with only 4% of seeds germinating at 20¬∞C ( Wang et al., 1996 ). Germination appears to be favoured by acidic conditions. Tazawa et al. (2021) reported 63% to 72% of germination in buffer solutions ranging from pH 4.6 to 6.4 at 30¬∞C under a 12-h light/dark cycle. At pH=7 germination percent decreased to 42%. Seed germination is higher in light than in darkness and decreases with burial depth ( Wang et al., 1996 ). Koarai and Shibayama (2001) reported over 90% of seedling emergence for seeds located within the top 2 mm of puddled soil. Kim and Mercado (1987) suggest that P. vaginalis seeds develop a light requirement for germination when buried for 1 month or longer at depths of greater than 1 cm. Noda and Eguchi (1965) found that seedling emergence is greater in early planted ricefields in Japan and, with early planting, seedling emergence was higher in saturated soils than in dry soils. They also found that the patterns of seed germination varied greatly according to moisture levels. In submerged conditions, the majority of seedlings emerged within a short space of time, the peak germination being between 15 and 25 days. In contrast, in saturated or dried soils, germination occurred gradually throughout the season.

Physiology and Phenology Pontederia vaginalis behaves as an annual or pseudoannual in flooded ricefields, but can also grow as a perennial in constantly flooded conditions ( Holm et al., 1977 ). In the Northern hemisphere, flowering has been reported from July to October ( Gupta, 1968 ; Kunii and Okibe, 1999 ; Les, 2020 ), but it can also occur throughout the year in the Philippines ( Merrill, 1912 ). In Australia, flowers have been recorded from February to May ( Aston, 1987 ). Seeds are produced within 4 to 5 weeks after flowering ( Wang et al., 1996 ). Submergence of fruits after seed initiation has been reported to be beneficial for seed development ( Gupta, 1968 ). The seeds remain in deep dormancy when kept under air-dried conditions, but dormancy is broken when they are dipped in cool water for over 2 months ( Wang et al., 1996 ). Environmental requirements Pontederia vaginalis is found mainly in tropical and humid subtropical climates, but can also tolerate cool-temperate climates. The plants root in mud, with their upper parts growing above water, so they require a shallow water environment to grow. They can be found in seasonal or permanent waters in open or shaded sites ( Aston, 1987 ; Les, 2020 ).
