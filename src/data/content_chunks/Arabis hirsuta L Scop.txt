Arabis hirsuta(L.) Scop. - Hairy Rock-cress  -Cruciferae / Brassicaceae© Roy Anderson© Stan Beesley© Robert Northridge(Map updated: March 2008)A small plant, usually less than six inches in height with a basal rosette of undivided leaves and small white flowers.This species occurs principally in two areas and habitats: around the coast on sandhills and inland on rocky ground around the limestone areas of Co. Fermanagh.The species includesArabis brownii, a minor variant which has been treated as a separate species by some botanists, but only differs fromA. hirsutaby being much less hairy.All names:Arabis hirsuta(L.)
