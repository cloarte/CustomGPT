 Habitat: 
 Reproduccion: Z. serrata grows predominantly within warm- and cool-temperate climate. Precipitation is concentrated in early summer to early autumn as rainfall, and additionally in winter as snowfall in some regions. Z. serrata is grown in average, medium wet, well-drained, acidic, clay, sandy soils. Trees grow differently according to soil fertility and permeability, preferring rich and moist loams, such as the lower slopes and dells ( Asami and Okuda, 1999 ). Established trees are drought tolerant. Z. serrate slightly prefer south-facing, gentle to intermediate slopes, or hollows.
