In Mediterranean climates, it can be a successful invader where land has been disturbed by grazing or construction. With continued land disturbance in particular climatic conditions (wet winters and dry summers), barley grass can persist and become dominant. The grass is of high forage value early in its growing season, but the seed awn has proven harmful to stock.  as a common spring weed in cities and towns, along roads, fencerows, ditches, rubbish dumps, disturbed soils, and in and about croplands.[7]Hordeum leporinumis ubiquitous in annual pastures of southern Australia that are characterized by a Mediterranean climate.
