Leaves usually opposite, rarely alternate or fasciculate, simple, often connate at the base, sessile or subsessile, entire; stipules interpetiolar, silvery scarious, rarely absent. Inflorescence an axillary or terminal cymose head or cluster, flowers rarely solitary terminal. Flowers minute, bisexual, actinomorphic, often perigynous, green or white; bracteate, bracts mostly scarious. Sepals 5, rarely 4, imbricate, persistent.
