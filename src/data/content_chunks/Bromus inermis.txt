 Reproduccion: Genetics The chromosome number reported for B. inermis varies from 2n = 14, 28, 56 ( Flora of China Editorial Committee, 2023 ). Reproductive Biology Bromus inermis is a self-incompatible grass. It reproduces by seed and through rhizome development. Plants are cross-pollinated, flower production is synchronous, and pollination may occur between plants up to 50 m apart. Seed production is variable, with plants producing between 156 and 10,080 viable seeds. This species can also reproduce vegetatively through rhizome development which begins 3 weeks to 6 months after germination ( Howard, 1996 ; DiTomaso et al., 2013 ; GISD, 2023 ). Physiology and Phenology Bromus inermis may grow and remain green year-round in warmer areas, but in colder climates it could be dormant in winter and, in general, the reproductive phenology is delayed in northern latitudes and high elevations. In areas across the Great Plains, the growth phase of B. inermis begins in early spring and continues into the late fall with flowering often occurring between May and June and seeds maturing in early to late summer. It may regrow and reflower in the fall if moisture is sufficient ( Howard, 1996 ; DiTomaso et al., 2013 ; GISD, 2023 ). In China, it
