Unlike many other moths, the mating system is based on female attraction by males using both olfactory and acoustical signals. Males produce short ultrasonic pulses when wings are fluttered. This call stimulates wing fanning by the virgin females, which produces a low-frequency sound that is detected by males. In the laboratory, the larvae can be maintained on artificial diets containing honey and wheat products ( Ellis et al., 2013 ). Cannibalism has been reported in crowded conditions, when larvae cannot enlarge their tunnels without disturbing other larvae ( Nielsen and Brister, 1979 ; Williams, 1997 ).
