The effects of three nematode species, T. crassicaudatus , P. penetrans and Meloidogyne naasi , on top growth of the host was also found additive. Furthermore, T. crassicaudatus (= T. agri ) was found to inhabit population increase of M. naasi in the host due to nematode-nematode competition but not vice versa. Also, high populations of T. crassicaudatus (= T. agri ) and Paratrichodorus minor (= Nanidorus minor ) together reduced infection by M. naasi on red clover ( Sikora et al., 1979 ).
