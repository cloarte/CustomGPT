The larval galleries in the cambial region form a zig-zag pattern and at various locations broaden out into wide cavities that score deeply into both the bark and outer sapwood (Stebbing, 1914; Beeson, 1941). Stebbing (1914) stated that the portion of the larval gallery constructed on the sapwood surface is about 30 cm long and that the cavities are about 7.5 cm wide. Larvae usually chew 2-4 holes through the bark at various locations during their tunneling and use these holes to eject some of their frass and wood shavings (Stebbing, 1914; Beeson, 1941; Gupta and Tara, 2013 ). At times various tree exudates also drip from these frass-ejection holes (Rahman and Khan, 1942; Gupta and Tara, 2013 ). Gupta and Sharma (2015) noted active expulsion of frass from infested trees from April through November, peaking during August to October.
