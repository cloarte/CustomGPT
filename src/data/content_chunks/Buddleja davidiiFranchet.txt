Buddleja davidiiFranchet - Butterfly-bush  -Buddlejaceae© Paul Hackney© Paul Hackney(Map updated: March 2008)The buddleia or butterfly bush is a plant of Chinese origin brought to Europe in the nineteenth century for use as a garden shrub. It is particularly attractive to butterflies.B. davidiispreads by producing abundant viable seed and is now established and widely naturalized in old quarries (especially limestone quarries) and on masonry walls and waste ground in urban situations.This is the onlyBuddlejaspecies which is naturalized in Northern Ireland althoughB. globosa, which is fairly frequently cultivated, has very occasionally been found as a garden escape.All names:Buddleja davidiiFranchetNBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
