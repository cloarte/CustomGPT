Salix humboldtiana flowers in September and October, with pollination through small insects and fruiting occurring from February to April ( Lorenzi, 2002 ). Seeds are dispersed by wind. Vegetative reproduction is common in the Salicaceae family, from branches that root when in contact with the ground. This species only reproduces vegetatively in Australia as female plants are not present ( Spencer, 1997 ) and flowering rarely occurs; when present, the male catkins are 3-10 cm long and 6-10 mm wide ( Baker, 2009 ).
