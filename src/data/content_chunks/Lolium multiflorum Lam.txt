Lolium multiflorumLam. - Italian Rye-grass  -Gramineae / Poaceae© Graham Day© Alan Hill(Map updated: March 2008)Italian rye-grass is very similar to perennial rye-grass (Lolium perenne) but the flower spikelets carry long bristles called `awns'. It is not native and is only found as a fodder crop or as a casual escape from cultivation on open or disturbed ground. It can hybridize with meadow fescue (Festuca pratensis), but the resulting hybrid (XFestulolium braunii) has rarely been found in Northern Ireland.All names:Lolium multiflorumLam.;Lolium perenne ssp. multiflorumLam.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
