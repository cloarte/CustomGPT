Purple seed stain is also known as purple blotch, purple speck, purple spot or lavender spot.Symptoms and SignsCercospora leaf blight may show up at the beginning of seed set. Initially, light purple spots develop on the top surface of leaves exposed to light, typically located on the upper half of the plants. Later on, these spots develop on both sides of the leaves. The spots expand and the color develops into reddish-purple or bronze. Infected leaves appear leathery or sunburned. Discolored spots coalesce forming larger necrotic lesions which may eventually die and drop to the ground. Reddish-purple lesions may also show on leaf stalks, stems and pods. Infected seeds develop pink to purple spots ranging from small specks to large blotches covering the seeds.Foliar symptoms of cercospora leaf blightPurple seed stainDisease ImpactsSeeds with high percentage of seed coat staining have lower oil content and higher protein content compared to healthy seeds. Purple seed stain is also known as purple blotch, purple speck, purple spot or lavender spot.Symptoms and SignsCercospora leaf blight may show up at the beginning of seed set. Initially, light purple spots develop on the top surface of leaves exposed to light, typically located on the upper half of the plants. Later on, these spots develop on both sides of the leaves. The spots expand and the color develops into reddish-purple or bronze. Infected leaves appear leathery or sunburned. Discolored spots coalesce forming larger necrotic lesions which may eventually die and drop to the ground. Reddish-purple lesions may also show on leaf stalks, stems and pods. Infected seeds develop pink to purple spots ranging from small specks to large blotches covering the seeds.Foliar symptoms of cercospora leaf blightPurple seed stainDisease ImpactsSeeds with high percentage of seed coat staining have lower oil content and higher protein content compared to healthy seeds. When planted, stained seeds generally have lower germination rate, undergo delayed germination and produce stunted seedlings. Purple seed stains do not reduce the yield directly. When planted, stained seeds generally have lower germination rate, undergo delayed germination and produce stunted seedlings. Purple seed stains do not reduce the yield directly. However, seed lots with high percentage of purple seed stain may fail seed certification.Life Cycle and EpidemiologyThe pathogen overwinters on plant debris in the soil. Infection occurs in early flowering but often shows no symptoms until the soybean reaches the R4 developmental stage (full pod).Cercospora kikuchiiproduces spores under conditions of high humidity and temperaturesbetween 73-80ËšF. Once a plant is infected, seed infection is not limited by weather  factors. ==Management Approaches==Cultural PracticesCrop rotation and tillage will reduce inoculum level.Host Plant ResistanceSoybean varieties vary in their susceptibility to this disease. The resistance against leaf and seed infection is currently thought to be controlled by different genes, thus resistance against leaf infection may not necessarily confer resistance against seed infection.Chemical ControlIf seed lots with a high percentage of purple seed stain are to be used, fungicidal seed treatment is recommended.
