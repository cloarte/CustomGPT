CREPIS TECTORUM (L.) Asteraceae Common name: Hawkbeard Eco&Biol. Annual or biennial. Root: a tap root, branched. Stem: 15-60 cm tall, dentated from base, originate as one from base but branched at the top, somewhat hairy. Leaves: broad at the base clasping the step with the pair of earlike lobes at the base, toothed, lance shaped, sometimes pinnatefied. Inflorescence: like corymb, flower heads yellow, small, bracts of the head are coarse and stiff. Seed dark brown or black, 3.5-6 mm long, 0.4-0.5 mm wide, with 10 corrugations or ribs. Flowering time: June - September Distribution: Widely distributed in crop fields, waste places and lawns. Most common in forest zone of Mongolia, particularly in alternate fallow fields. Control measures: Timely tillage in alternate fallow fields is necessary to avoid its infestation in crop fields. Countries: Mongolia , Back to Database of Weed Species in Crops and Countries
