 Sintomas: D. africanus does not cause visible lesions on the roots of groundnuts, but affects the appearance, and sometimes the weight and untimely germination, of the pods and seeds ( Venter et al., 1991 ). Infected hulls also show greyish-black to brown necrotic tissue at the point of connection with the peg, and in broad bands along the longitudinal veins.
