Sedum rupestreL. - Reflexed Stonecrop  -Crassulaceae(Map updated: March 2008)A succulent perennial with thick linear leaves carried in rosettes or tufts at the ends of the branches. There are no dead leaves on the stems below these clusters (in contrast to the similarSedum forsterianum). Flowers are yellow, star-shaped, produced in heads.An introduction from the European continent. Occasional on old stone walls.Often confused withSedum forsterianumwhich grows in the same sort of habitat.All names:Sedum rupestreL.;Sedum reflexumL.;Sedum glaucumHaw., non Lam.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
