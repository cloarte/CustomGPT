Very little information is available on the reproductive biology of S. junceus. Reproduction is by seed and plant divisions ( Institute for Regional Conservation, 2020 ). Sporobolus junceus is a C4 grass, adapted to fire-maintained longleaf pine savannahs ( Kirkman et al., 2004 ). Flowers and fruits are produced 7-9 weeks following a fire ( Canfield and Tanner, 1997 ). It is also reported as flowering all year ( Institute for Regional Conservation, 2020 ). Sporobolus junceus is a perennial grass ( Institute for Regional Conservation, 2020 ). There is very little detailed information about the environmental requirements of S. junceus.
