Groenlandia densa(L.) Fourr. - Opposite-leaved Pondweed  -Potamogetonaceae© Ralph Forbes(Map updated: March 2008)Resembling aPotamogetonspecies, but with opposite, not alternate, leaves. This is a totally-submerged pondweed of base-rich lakes. The only Northern Ireland site is Lough-na-Trosk in Co. Antrim, where it was discovered in 1971. This lake sits in a basin below some basaltic cliffs at about 290 metres altitude and has an alkaline pH.Two dots appear on the map because the lake falls within two adjacent hectads.All names:Groenlandia densa(L.)
