 Reproduccion: Genetics In the Pogostemon genus, x=16 or 17 is the fundamental chromosomal number ( Cherian and Kuriachan, 1993 ). There is substantial debate over patchouli's chromosomal count. While Lavania (1984) claimed that the patchouli chromosome count was 32, further research ( Tyagi and Bahl, 1990 ; Chen et al., 2009 ; Widoretno, 2016 ; Yan et al., 2016 ) revealed that the patchouli chromosome count was 64. It appears that the most prevalent patchouli cytotypic is 2n=4x=64. He et al. (2016 , 2018 ) examined and assembled the patchouli genome. The genome of this complex plant has a high heterozygosity and substantial repeat content. Due to the limitations of next-generation sequencing and chromosome anchoring technology, the two assembled genomes varied dramatically (1.15‚ÄâGb vs. 1.91‚ÄâGb) and no chromosome-level sequences were obtained. Reproductive Biology Patchouli is propagated by cuttings. Nursery is formed in a shady region throughout the growth season by planting 10 to 12 cm long cuttings at 10 x 10 cm spacing to preserve moisture in the seed beds ( Maiti et al., 2006 ). If all goes according to plan, 85-90% of cuttings that have been developing roots for 2 weeks will be ready for field planting in 6 to 8 weeks. Mulch is a useful tool for controlling excess water evaporation, which helps to regulate soil temperature and preserve soil moisture ( Nasruddin et al., 2019 ). Mulching is necessary to save soil and water in regions with irregular rainfall patterns. Patchouli plants require an adequate supply of nutrients at appropriate times. One method for patchouli plants to have strong leaves and abundant output is fertilization. The use of mulch and fertilizer with the right dose and time reduces the impact of water shortage on patchouli plants ( Nasruddin et al., 2019 ).
