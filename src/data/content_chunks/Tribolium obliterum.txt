The leaf blades are linear and narrow, 0.5-1 mm wide and 1-8 cm long (Linder & Davidse 1997), without cross venation. Plants in the genusTriboliumare bisexual with bisexual spikelets and hermaphrodite florets (Watson & Dallwitz 1992). It its native range, flowering occurs in the spring (September - November) (Anonymous 2001b). The genus contains ten species that occur in the Fynbos and Karoo and commonly are adventive (Watson & Dallwitz 1992).Tribolium obliterumis typically found at low altitudes on gravelly, well-drained soils (Linder & Davidse 1997).Tribolium obliterumis an invader in one small site in California (USA), and at sites in Australia, and on St. Helena and Ascension Islands in the Atlantic. There are no reports of it spreading rapidly in these areas (Linder & Davidse 1997). The CaliforniaTribolium obliterumpopulation is spreading in an eroded gully and in a restoration site where the soil was disturbed. Species in the genusTriboliumare mesophytic to xerophytic, common in open habitats, and do not like salty soils (Watson & Dallwitz 1992). RenvoizeCommon Names:Cape grassThe genus name,Tribolium, was derived from the Latintri, meaning three andbolus, meaning a fiery meteor in the form of an arrow. The genus name was possibly in reference to the bristly glumes surrounding the 3 florets (Hubbard 2001). Synonyms forTribolium obliterumare numerous including "Brizopyrum glomeratum" and "Poa glomerata". See the Missouri Botanical Gardens website (Anonymous 2001a) for a complete list. The common name "Cape grass" was coined by Hrusa (Hrusa 2001).Stewardship summaryTribolium obliterum(Hemsl.) Renvoize is a non-native grass believed to have arrived in California as an ornamental. The Plant Diagnostics Center of the California Department of Food and Agriculture has given the plant a Q rating, meaning that it is a quarantine plant and will be intercepted if sold in the U.S. until further evaluation. The quarantine rating is primarily because the behavior of the plant is unknown at this time (Hrusa 2001). In disturbed sites the impact ofT. obliterumis minimal, but it may compete with native bunchgrasses where they grow together (Hubbard 2001). Other species in the genusTriboliumcan be agricultural weeds, but are not considered invasive in natural areas (Hrusa 2001).Natural historyDescriptionTribolium obliterum(Hemsl.) Renvoize is a perennial grass (Poaceae). It has decumbent or erect culms 12-40 cm long, with 3-4 nodes (Linder & Davidse 1997). The leaf blades are linear and narrow, 0.5-1 mm wide and 1-8 cm long (Linder & Davidse 1997), without cross venation. Plants in the genusTriboliumare bisexual with bisexual spikelets and hermaphrodite florets (Watson & Dallwitz 1992). It its native range, flowering occurs in the spring (September - November) (Anonymous 2001b). The genus contains ten species that occur in the Fynbos and Karoo and commonly are adventive (Watson & Dallwitz 1992).Tribolium obliterumis typically found at low altitudes on gravelly, well-drained soils (Linder & Davidse 1997).Tribolium obliterumis an invader in one small site in California (USA), and at sites in Australia, and on St. Helena and Ascension Islands in the Atlantic. There are no reports of it spreading rapidly in these areas (Linder & Davidse 1997). The CaliforniaTribolium obliterumpopulation is spreading in an eroded gully and in a restoration site where the soil was disturbed. Species in the genusTriboliumare mesophytic to xerophytic, common in open habitats, and do not like salty soils (Watson & Dallwitz 1992). Susan Hubbard (Hubbard 2001) has observed thatT. obliterumprefers clear, flat areas and has been found along dirt roads and in other disturbed areas. Seeds may be spread by water.
