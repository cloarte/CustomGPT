Lathyrus linifolius(Reichard) Baessler - Bitter-vetch  -Leguminosae / Fabaceae© Graham Day© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)Bitter vetch is a native perennial species growing up to about 15 ins (40 cm) or more, found in heaths, woods, thickets, scrub etc. across Northern Ireland, from sea level to quite high altitudes in the mountains.  The leaves have 2 to 4 pairs of long-oval leaflets (sometimes very narrow or linear) and no tendrils; the flowers are in pairs on long stalks and are a dingy blue-purple, and are followed by long blackish pods containing up to about 10 seeds; the stem is winged.All names:Lathyrus linifolius(Reichard) Baessler;Lathyrus montanusBernh.;Lathyrus macrorrhizusWimm.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
