Ornithopus perpusillusL. - Bird's-foot  -Leguminosae / Fabaceae(Map updated: March 2008)Probably not a native species in Northern Ireland (the earliest record dates from as recently as 1924 and its habitats tend to be man-made such as quarries or sand pits), and not persistent at its recorded sites. The most recent report is from short turf in a sand pit near Ballykelly in 1982.It is a small prostrate annual with pinnate leaves, pale yellow or white flowers veined with red, and fruits consisting of a group of narrow beaded peapods resembling a bird's foot.All names:Ornithopus perpusillusL.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
