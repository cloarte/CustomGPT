Fallopia sachalinensis(F.Schmidt ex Maxim.) Ronse Decraene - Giant Knotweed  -Polygonaceae© Paul Hackney© Paul Hackney© Paul Hackney(Map updated: March 2008)© Paul HackneyThis is a vigorous perennial knotweed, forming huge clumps up to 10 feet high. It is similar to Japanese knotweed (Fallopia japonica), but rarer. Both male and female plants occur in Northern Ireland (unlikeF. japonicawhich only produces female flowers in Ireland) and its pollen can fertilizeF. japonicaflowers to produce a rare hybrid when the two species grow near together.A native of Korea, Japan and Sakhalin, introduced to Europe for gardens. It is found on riversides and waste ground, the products of discarded garden waste containing fragments of the plant.All names:Fallopia sachalinensis(F.Schmidt ex Maxim.)
