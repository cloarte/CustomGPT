Bromus hordeaceusL. - Soft-brome  -Gramineae / Poaceae© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)Soft brome is a rather variable annual or biennial plant, from about four inches to three feet tall, producing fat egg-shaped spikelets of flowers at the ends of  the panicle branches; rather soft to the touch. It can be found in a wide variety of habitats including meadows, stone walls, waste ground, roadside verges etc. in lowland situations.An alternative name found in older books isBromus mollis.All names:Bromus hordeaceusL.;Bromus hordeaceus sens. lat.L.;Bromus mollisL.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
