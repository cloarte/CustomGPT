In areas of South Dakota where hay quality and quantity are optimized by midsummer mowing the practice results in conversion to bluegrass dominance (Steuter pers. comm.). A similar pattern of increased bluegrass with summer mowing is exhibited in the Kansas Flint Hills (Launchbaugh and Owensby 1978).
