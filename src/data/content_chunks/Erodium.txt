 alfilaria, filaree, redstem filaree, and storkâ€™s bill. Seedling leaves (cotyledons) and first true leaves are deeply lobed and have long hairy petioles. Red stemmed filaree plants form a basal rosette. Rosette leaves are opposite, on petioles, and divided into 3 to 9 leaflets. In warmer climates, seeds germinate in fall, but not until midsummer the following year in colder climates when soil temperatures reach 69Â° during the day and 40Â° at night. Flowers form from early spring through summer and plants can produce seeds within 2 to 4 months after germinating. Seeds survive 1 â€“ 3 years in the soil and flushes of germination occur in response to soil disturbance. Red stem filaree can form extensive seed banks.Management ApproachesBiological ControlRed stemmed filaree has good forage value and can withstand heavy stocking rates. There is currently no biological control program for red stemmed filaree.Mechanical and Cultural ControlIn turf grass, mowing hoeing or digging up rosettes as soon as they are recognizable can be effective.
