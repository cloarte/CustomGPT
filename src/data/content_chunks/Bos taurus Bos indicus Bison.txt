Breadcrumb Home Trade Imports Live Animal Imports Importing Live Cattle, Bison and Germplasm into the United States Importing Live Cattle, Bison and Germplasm into the United States Last Modified:  March 22, 2024 USDA APHIS regulates the importation of all ruminants and their germplasm (embryos/oocytes, semen, cloning tissue) to prevent the spread of animal diseases. Ruminants include all animals which chew the cud, such as cattle, buffaloes, sheep, goats, deer, antelopes, camels, llamas and giraffes. USDA APHIS defines the following animals as bovine : Bos taurus , Bos indicus and Bison bison . Notice Regarding APHIS Live Animal Import and Third-Country Import Transit Permits (272.29 KB) . Please note that any animals and their germplasm transiting the United States must not transit countries with questionable disease statuses prior to reaching a U.S. port of entry. All transits require a contingency plan. Please submit your contingency plan with your permit application ( VS 17-129 (211.74 KB) ) to laipermits@usda.gov .
