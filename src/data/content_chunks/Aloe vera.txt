 Reproduccion: Genetics The chromosome number reported for A. vera is 2n=14 ( Flora of China Editorial Committee, 2017 ). As a result of the continuous vegetative propagation, the genetic variation within A. vera in cultivation seems to be rather small. Populations in China and India have been isolated for centuries and may harbour characteristics that could be useful elsewhere ( PROTA, 2024 ). Reproductive Biology Aloe vera flowers are bisexual and often pollinated by birds ( Sapre, 1975 ). In Africa, honeybees also play a role in pollination. A. vera flowers and fruits normally in Africa, but elsewhere fruit formation is rare. It is presumed that failure to set fruit is caused by pollen sterility and self-incompatibility ( PROTA, 2024 ). Physiology and Phenology Aloe vera belongs to a large class of plants known as ‚Äòxeroids‚Äô characterized for its ability to close its stomata completely to avoid loss of water. This adaptation allows plants to survive long and extreme drought periods ( Akinyele and Odiyi, 2007 ). The ability of aloe species to fix carbon dioxide at night and photosynthesize with closed stomata during the day, thus minimizing water loss, is also called Crassulacean acid metabolism (CAM). This, alongside their succulent stems and leaves, and the presence of a thick cuticle, makes them well adapted to dry conditions ( PROTA, 2024 ). Longevity Aloe vera is a perennial, long-lived, succulent plant. It takes 4-5 years to mature and can live up to 25 years ( Akinyele and Odiyi, 2007 ). This, alongside their succulent stems and leaves, and the presence of a thick cuticle, makes them well adapted to dry conditions ( PROTA, 2024 ).
