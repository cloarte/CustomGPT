Medicago sativaL. - Lucerne  -Leguminosae / Fabaceae© Graham Day(Map updated: March 2008)A perennial plant of southern European origin, sown as an animal fodder crop and known to farmers as alfalfa or lucerne. Occasionally found on marginal or waste ground as an escape, sometimes persisting for many years. It is a tall species (up to about 3 ft or 90 cm) with trefoil leaves and compact spikes of blue or violet flowers.
