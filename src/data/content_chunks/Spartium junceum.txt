The fragrant yellow flowers are borne in loose terminal racemes, unlike those ofCytisus scopariuswhich are usually solitary in the axils. Individual flowers are 2-2.5 cm long.  The banner and keel are longer than the wings, and the keel is pubescent along its lower edge.  The calyx is split above, hence one-lipped, with 5 minute teeth. These methods are highly selective and permit weeds to be removed without damage to surrounding native vegetation. The Bradley Method is one sensible approach to manual control of weeds.[10]This method consists of hand weeding selected small areas of infestation in a specific sequence, starting with the best stands of native vegetation (those with the least extent of weed infestation) and working towards those stands with the worst weed infestation.
