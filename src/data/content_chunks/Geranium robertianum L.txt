Geranium robertianumL. - Herb-robert  -Geraniaceae© Fiona McCrory© Fiona McCrory© Roy Anderson(Map updated: March 2008)Herb Robert is one of the commonest of our native annual wild plants in a wide variety of habitats, but especially in shaded hedge banks, woodland, on walls, seaside shingle banks and on rock faces.It is easily recognised by its small pinkish-purple flowers and the characteristic fern-like leaves. The stems are usually dark red, with swollen nodes, normally lying horizontally on the ground, but sometimes clambering up through other plants.The closely-relatedG. purpureum(Little Robin) does not occur in Northern Ireland.All names:Geranium robertianumL.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
