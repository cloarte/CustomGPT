Bean, Moth
Mat, Dew gram
Leguminosae
Phaseolus aconitifolius
Jacq.
Source:
Magness et al. 1971
This bean is, a trailing plant, with stems up to 2 feet, covered with stiffhairs. Pods are small, about 2 inches long, nearly round and glabrous. Seedsare small. Moth bean is cultivated for food in South Asia and for forage, butis rarely grown in the U.S. Seeds are used as dry beans.
