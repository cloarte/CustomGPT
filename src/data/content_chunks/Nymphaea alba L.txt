Nymphaea albaL. - White Water-lily  -Nymphaeaceae© Ralph Forbes© Ian Dodkins© Ralph Forbes(Map updated: March 2008)© Graham DayThe white water-lily grows in lakes, mainly concentrated in the southern half of Northern Ireland. It is not rare, but is rather less frequent than the yellow water-lily (Nuphar lutea).It is entirely aquatic, rooted in the mud at the lake bottom but with its rounded leaves and white many-petalled flowers floating on the surface. It will grow in less nutrient-rich water thanNuphar lutea.All names:Nymphaea albaL.;Nymphaea occidentalis(Ostenf.) Moss;Castalia alba(L.)
