TBRV, as other viruses, is an obligate intracellular parasite that requires for propagation specialized host cells supplying the complex metabolic and biosynthetic machinery. TBRV is durable and can remain infective for long periods of time. In laboratory experiments, the virus was found to remain infective after a 10-min exposure to 58¬∞C (136¬∞F) but failed to infect after exposure to 62¬∞C (144¬∞F) ( Smith, 1946 ). After 6 years in storage, TBRV-infected seed of Capsella bursa-pastoris (common chickenweed) and Stellaria media (shepherd's purse) germinated to produce virus-infected seedlings ( Lister and Murant, 1967 ). TBRV is distributed worldwide; the virus can be found in both moderate and tropical climate conditions.
