 Reproduccion: Chromosome number is not available. Hybridization tests with closely related Meloidogyne species have not been performed. Meloidogyne artiellia reproduces asexually, but has the ability to reproduce sexually when conditions are appropriate ( Giorgi et al., 2002 ). Adults have been observed after 14-18 days following root penetration and optimal temperatures for nematode development (15-25췈C). Hatching starts at temperatures between 5 and 10췈C, but the optimal is at temperatures between 15 and 25췈C. Eggs will not hatch under extended dry periods but may persist in soil or dry roots awaiting more favourable moist soil conditions. Juveniles may become inactive under dry conditions. Longevity is not determined, but other female Meloidogyne species could be alive in the host for several months. Males do not feed on roots, are vermiform and move out of the root after they complete their development and they survive in the soil for several weeks. Humidity and optimal temperature are needed for hatching and host root penetration. It is not clear if root exudates stimulate the hatching, but eggs in egg masses hatch spontaneously in water if temperature is above 5췈C. At the end of the host crop, populations reach the maximal level. Each female lays in the egg mass from 110 to 596 eggs ( Fern치ndez et al., 2005 ). The reproductive fitness, determined by the number of eggs per egg mass, was significantly influenced by the host plant (wheat is a better host than chickpea), nematode population, as well as the host plant 칑 nematode population interaction ( Fern치ndez et al., 2005 ). Nematodes must interact closely with the host plant in order to create the feeding site. There has been a difference detected in reproduction depending on the host ( Fern치ndez et al., 2005 ).
