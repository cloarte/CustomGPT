Their common name is based on the fact that adults reflexively bleed an oily substance called cantharidin, familiarly called "spanish fly". This substance is used for defense, and can cause blisters to form on skin and mucous membranes to bleed. Adults have a fairly simple lifestyle, feeding on plant tissue (leaves and flowers), eating pollen or drinking nectar (some even have elongate, sucking mouthparts in addition to biting mandibles). Most larval blister beetles, however, have a complex mode of life called hypermetamorphosis. This occurs because the larvae are parasites (and kleptoparasites - ones that steal food from their hosts) and need to adapt to the many actions they must take. First they are active insects (calledtriungulins), with legs and a hardened body. These larvae either actively find the nests of their host, or hitch rides back to the nest on adults. Some even mimic flowers and produce scents to attract the adult host. Once in the nest, the larvae transform into more grub-like insects, that sit and become fat while feeding on the food stores, eggs, larvae and pupae of the host. Diets of Meloidae include food stores and young of bees and wasps, and grasshopper eggs. Blister beetles are most often found in grasslands/fields or in arid regions.Economic Importance:This family can be beneficial or pestiferous. Larvae of some attack grasshoppers and locusts, helping to control pest populations, and adults may be important pollinators in some areas by visiting flowers and transporting pollen. On the other hand, blister beetles can be detrimental to agriculture and human endeavors. Adults have a fairly simple lifestyle, feeding on plant tissue (leaves and flowers), eating pollen or drinking nectar (some even have elongate, sucking mouthparts in addition to biting mandibles). Most larval blister beetles, however, have a complex mode of life called hypermetamorphosis. This occurs because the larvae are parasites (and kleptoparasites - ones that steal food from their hosts) and need to adapt to the many actions they must take. First they are active insects (calledtriungulins), with legs and a hardened body. These larvae either actively find the nests of their host, or hitch rides back to the nest on adults. Some even mimic flowers and produce scents to attract the adult host. Once in the nest, the larvae transform into more grub-like insects, that sit and become fat while feeding on the food stores, eggs, larvae and pupae of the host. Diets of Meloidae include food stores and young of bees and wasps, and grasshopper eggs. Blister beetles are most often found in grasslands/fields or in arid regions.Economic Importance:This family can be beneficial or pestiferous.
