RAPHANUS RAPHANISTRATRUM L. Family: Brassicaceae Common name: White charlock Ecol. & Biol: Annual. Root: a tap root. Stem: 20-70 cm. high, often flexous, densly covered with coarse hairs at base, sparsely so above. Leaves: lower leaves lyrate pinnatisect, lateral lobes 4-6 paired, all leaves coarse and hirsute. Inflorescence: flowers - petals yellow, rarely white or with a violet tinge, fruit - a pod (siliqua) 3-8 cmm long and 3-4 mm broad, pointed at tip, Seeds - oval to round brownish. Propagation by seeds. Flowering time: July - September. Distribution : An occasional weed in vegetables throughout Mongolia. More common on frequently cultivated and irrigated fields. Control measures: Manual weeding particularly late in the season is useful for avoiding the aeneration of seed bank of this weed.
