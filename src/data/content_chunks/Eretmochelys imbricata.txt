retmochelys imbricata (Linnaeus, 1766)
Museums Field Guide apps species profiles – vertebrates
Brief description A large green sea turtle with a prominent beak.
Description A large-sized green sea turtle. Overall body colour is green above with brown, red, or black variegations on the shell. Head is very distinct, with a large beak. Forearms are modified into large flippers. Maximum length 100 cm.
Biology This species can be quite common along coasts. Adults are commonly found foraging on sponges in coral reefs. They rarely nest in Australian waters, but have been known to occasionally nest in the Gulf of Carpentaria and Torres Strait regions. Up to 50 eggs are laid in a season. This species is listed as Vulnerable under the Territory Parks and Wildlife Act.
