Ranunculus fluitansLam. - River Water-crowfoot  -Ranunculaceae© Neville McKee© Neville McKee© Neville McKee(Map updated: March 2008)© Neville McKeeThis is one of the so-called 'water-crowfoots' which are aquatic white-flowered relatives of buttercups growing in streams and rivers. It is similar toRanunculus penicillatus, a very common aquatic plant of rivers, but with somewhat longer submerged leaves and no floating leaves. It is confined to one single river in Ireland: The Six Mile Water, County Antrim, where it is abundant, and hybridizes withRanunculus penicillatus.All names:Ranunculus fluitansLam.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
