Myrosin cells present in bark of branches and in inflorescence, producing glucosinolates. Leaves alternate, imparipinnate; leaflets petiolulate, opposite or lower ones alternate, entire; veins pinnate; stipules absent. Racemes terminal, erect. Flowers bisexual, zygomorphic. Calyx broadly campanulate, 5-lobed. Petals 5, free, imbricate, unequal, clawed, adnate from middle to upper part of calyx tube. Stamens 8, attached to thin, annular nectary disc at base of petals, shorter than petals; filaments filiform, hairy, especially in basal 1/2. Pistil 1; ovary superior, sessile, 3-5-loculed; placentation axile; ovules 2 per locule, pendulous; style longer than stamens; stigma capitate, minute. Fruit a capsule; valves 3-5, thick walled, dehiscent. Seeds oblong; endosperm absent.One species: S China, N Thailand, N Vietnam.The taxonomic placement of the Bretschneideraceae has been enigmatic, with alliances postulated among such widely divergent families as Capparaceae, Fabaceae, Hippocastanaceae, Moringaceae, and Sapindaceae.
