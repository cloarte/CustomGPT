 formation of raised knots or galls primarily on young branches and stems. Infection can occasionally occur on leaves, roots, and fruits. At the onset of infection, the knots begin as pale-green, small smooth lumps at nodes or on barks. Knots gradually turn greenish-brown or brown and hard with a cracked surface. These knots can develop to form rough-surfaced galls more than 2.5 cm in diameter. Within the gall tissues, the pathogen produces bacteria-filled cavities (2,6).Overtime, the knots crack and become partly necrotized. About 6 to 8 months after formation, knots begin to decay and die (2). Severe infections, where the knot partially or completely encircles the branch, can result in dwarfism, defoliation, or death of affected limbs.  Knots on immature plants can weaken the structure of the tree by partially girdling the branches (6). Even moderate infection can result in reduced yield and size of olives, which translates into a significant economic impact (4). Although rare, fruit infections can result in deformation or circular brown spots about 0.5 to 2.5 mm in diameter.  formation of raised knots or galls primarily on young branches and stems. Infection can occasionally occur on leaves, roots, and fruits. At the onset of infection, the knots begin as pale-green, small smooth lumps at nodes or on barks. Knots gradually turn greenish-brown or brown and hard with a cracked surface. These knots can develop to form rough-surfaced galls more than 2.5 cm in diameter. Within the gall tissues, the pathogen produces bacteria-filled cavities (2,6).Overtime, the knots crack and become partly necrotized. About 6 to 8 months after formation, knots begin to decay and die (2). Severe infections, where the knot partially or completely encircles the branch, can result in dwarfism, defoliation, or death of affected limbs.  Knots on immature plants can weaken the structure of the tree by partially girdling the branches (6). Even moderate infection can result in reduced yield and size of olives, which translates into a significant economic impact (4). Although rare, fruit infections can result in deformation or circular brown spots about 0.5 to 2.5 mm in diameter.
