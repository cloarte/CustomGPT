Equisetum fluviatileL. - Water Horsetail  -Equisetaceae© John Wilde(Map updated: March 2008)This horsetail is very common in ponds, lakes, ditches, fens and other aquatic situations. It stands about one or two feet tall (sometimes more) and grows vertically up from a horizontal rhizome (creeping stem) which is buried in the mud below the water. It can be either completely unbranched, or have regular whorls of short branches. There is a large central hollow down the centre of the shoots. Blackish cones are produced at the tips of the shoots.All names:Equisetum fluviatileL.;Equisetum limosumL.NBN Atlas mapping:Species account: NBN Atlas UK Species Observations databaseiNaturalist:Species account: iNaturalist World Species Observations database«« Return to previous pageNext Species »
